What is alternative data?

Alternative data is a term used to describe anything outside of traditional financial data that can be used to find unique insights into a company’s operations and performance trends. Increasingly, these types of data are sought by investors as a valuable source of timely information to aid in investment decisions. Why is alternative data useful?

Alternative data gives traders an information edge over the market. This creates opportunities for generating alpha, or excess investment return relative to a market index or other benchmark.

 

One of the most touted benefits of alternative data is that it updates frequently, giving more timely information than traditional data sources. For example, satellite imagery of daily parking lot capacity can shed light on traffic to a company’s stores. Social media data can highlight changes in consumers’ sentiment for a product, brand, or company. These types of data can allow investors to gather more granular insights into a company’s performance, illuminating potential investment opportunities.

 

Although there are exciting potential benefits, gathering and interpreting these large and complex data sets are often outside the capabilities of individual investors. Collecting, analyzing, and interpreting the data often requires the expertise of experienced data scientists.

 

Who uses alternative data?

Paired with advances in Big Data over the past decade, investors’ quest for alpha has resulted in an explosion of demand for alternative data sources. At the same time, new data brokers and other intermediaries have emerged to provide data cleaning and interpretation services for alternative data.

However, only hedge funds and professional money-managers have been able to take full advantage of this explosion of alternative data. Existing providers charge users tens, even hundreds of thousands of dollars a year for access to their data.

This exclusivity creates an unlevel playing field. Wall Street power houses can easily access the data and build in-house analytics teams whose sole purpose is to collect, manage, analyze, and interpret alternative data. Meanwhile, this same data has been almost completely inaccessible to ordinary people.
