In this letter, we’re going to take another look at the relationship between WSB discussion and market activity at a stock-level. We’ve picked 3 stocks that were among the most popular in the last 30 days, and we’ve examined them to determine the relationship between WSB, share price, and volume.

BlackBerry (NYSE: BB)

BlackBerry has been under the radar for years but recently the stock has been unpredictable due to high short interest, diversification in its business plan, and WSB popularity. On the week of January 25th alone, we collected 65,201 mentions of BB (one every 9 seconds). Below is a graph showing WSB sentiment towards BlackBerry, and BlackBerry’s share price on that week:

Apart from some discrepancies, WSB sentiment and BB’s price share a positive correlation. WSB sentiment foreshadowed price fluctuations in multiple instances. BB printed a weekly high of $28.35 before ending the week on a 23.65% loss. Sentiment followed the price movement as we counted 1469 “calls”, compared to 3665 “puts”,

This indicates that among other factors, sentiment on certain socks will influence price changes. With millions of eyes looking at the forum for investment guidance, it’s evident that what the sentiment of the stock is, the price will follow suit.

Nokia (NYSE: NOK)

Another centerpiece in the ongoing war was Nokia which saw a total volume of 2.89b between the 25th and 29th of January. High volume would lead to high volatility with the stock price fluctuating over 150%. Below we consolidated the sentiment and price into 15-minute intervals:

WSB sentiment did not deviate a lot except for:

  • When the price broke $5.00 for the first time
  • When the price peaked at its high of $8.81
  • When the price fell below $5.00

To further investigate, we compiled data and identified support and resistance barriers for WSB sentiment towards BB and highlighted them in the graph below. These barriers incorporate standard deviation and SMA based on previous sentiment data. This technique allows users to estimate an approximate range of variation.

When the sentiment towards Nokia on WSB broke its top-end resistance marker, Nokia’s price eclipsed the $5 threshold , a valuation not seen since August. This example would repeat itself twice more throughout the week. While many elements contribute to the price fluxes, the data suggests that WSB sentiment is a tangible metric that can have actual sway on the price of certain stocks.

GameStop Corp. (NYSE: GME)

And how we write this letter without at least giving GameStop an honorary mention? After all, year-to-date, GameStop’s volume has been a cumulative 1.63 billion, just 73 million short of its entire 2020 volume (not even accounting for stock weight). Below is a graph representing GME’s volume and WSB discussion data for the 27th to the 29th of January:

While on this 15-minute interval there is a positive correlation of 0.498 between these 2 variables, typically, the stock volume fluctuates first, and consequently, WSB discussions count increases. Conclusion

Of course, you could debate the validity of some of the arguments proposed in this report and whether WSB has as meaningful effects on stocks as argued above. Ultimately this is only one argument and perhaps you interpret the data differently. However, it would be difficult to argue that if you had a strong position, bull or bearish, in any company that you shouldn’t keep at least one eye on WSB chatter. It might save you a big headache.
