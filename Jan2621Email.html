How effective was our Political Beta data at predicting the stock market impact of the Presidential election?

Political Beta Revisited

 

Last September we introduced Political Beta, a new method for predicting how different stocks would be affected by election outcomes. 

 

To measure Political Beta for the 2020 election, we tracked the correlation between a stock's daily price changes and the daily changes in presidential candidates' election odds. These odds were calculated based on trading on the PredictIt betting market, where over 100,000 users bought and sold contracts on the outcome of the election. This gave us a "Trump Beta" and "Biden Beta" for each company, which represented the correlation of their stock return with each candidate's election odds.

 

We predicted that companies with a high Biden Beta would outperform the market if Biden was elected, while Trump Beta companies would outperform if Trump won.

 

With the election decided and President Biden in office, we can now measure the performance of Political Beta and see how different industries have been impacted by the election result. 

Post-Election Performance

Given Biden's victory, we would expect stocks with a positive "Biden Beta" to have outperformed those with a positive "Trump Beta" after the election outcome became more clear.

 

Looking at the data, that is exactly what we see. Since last August, there is a 12% spread between the weighted returns of S&P 500 companies with a positive Biden Beta and companies with a positive Trump Beta.

Biden Beta companies underperformed on Election Day, but have been consistently beating the market in the weeks leading up to the inauguration.

 

Looking from Election Night to Inauguration Day across all companies, we find that average returns have been generally higher among stocks with higher Biden Betas.

With that in mind, let's take a closer look at which industries seem to have been impacted the most by the incoming Biden Administration.

Industry Analysis

Uranium

After scraping 1000's of companies and 100+ industries, uranium was the 3rd best-performing industry from 9/25/20 to the present. Centrus Energy Corp (LEU), and Energy Fuels (UUUU) have returned 161.35% and 148.39% respectively in that period alone. All uranium-based companies we examined shared a negative trumpBeta rating between -0.04 and -0.50.

So why is this happening? 

In December a new bipartisan bill was introduced called the Nuclear Infrastructure Act which aims to increase the investment, and reduce regulation regarding the nuclear industry in the US which already is worth $60b annually, supports 500,000 jobs, and produces 20% of domestic energy (RNS, 2021). 

 

The second reason is simply a supply and demand issue. COVID-19 resulted in several major industry suppliers having to scale back uranium mining operations. To make the perfect storm, 53 nuclear reactors are under construction with another 300 proposed and 450 are currently operational globally. With all these factors combined the outlook is positive and we'll be keeping an eye on it.

Cannabis

Predictably the Cannabis industry had an adverse relationship with the Trump administration. Accordingly, after election day Cannabis companies enjoyed renewed growth as investor sentiment improved. The graph below visualizes the stock price percent change of Canopy Growth (WEED), Aurora Cannabis (ACB), NewAge (NBEV), and GW Pharmaceuticals (GWPH). All 4 companies had very negative Trump Betas prior to Election Day.

Despite this, investors should focus not only on the existing publicly traded companies in the cannabis industry, but also on the potential for new companies to be traded:

 

Presently, marijuana is prohibited by the federal government making it arduous for companies in the industry to go public, resulting in many seeking Canadian based exchanges. With Democrats controlling the House, Senate, and White House, some analysts anticipate the $50bn industry to have access to US-based exchanges as soon as 24-months from now. 

Pharmaceuticals 

The healthcare and pharmaceutical industry has been able to maintain a favorable relationship with President Trump during his presidency and this resulted in a positive Trump Beta for Johnson & Johnson (JNJ), Eli Lilly and Company (LLY), Merck & Co (MRK), and Pfizer (PFE). Therefore It should come as a surprise that pharmaceutical companies have performed strongly since the election, and are within hands-reach of all-time highs.

The graph shows that with the exception of Merck (MRK), all of these companies have had stock price growth since the election. One theory behind this is that The Biden Administration intends to spend tens of billions of additional dollars to subsidize funding for the poorest Americans, expanding the potential market of pharmaceutical companies. Biden's approach is different from Trump's which focused on the reduction of drug prices. In short: With less price regulation and more healthcare spending, these companies will likely benefit for the foreseeable future. 

Big Tech

It’s no secret Big Tech has had a tenuous relationship with President Trump and foreign governments, subject to billion-dollar fines, senate hearings, and public scrutiny. This hasn’t stopped Google (GOOG), Amazon (AMZN), Facebook (FB), and Microsoft (MSFT) from immense valuation growth.

Silicon Valley has been working overtime to increase their representation in DC, achieving new positions in the DOJ, FTC, and Biden's own advisory board. One example is Biden's pick for Secretary of State, Antony Blinken, Biden's pick for Secretary of State who is co-founded a consulting company that has clients including Google and Palantir to name a few. No doubt software and data analytic based companies have significant hurdles ahead of them to overcome, however with their foot in the door in Washington, and millions in lobbying annually, the outlook to prevent reduce their monopoly is bleak.

Conclusion

Political Beta proved to be an effective tool for predicting how companies would be affected by the Presidential election outcome. In future elections, it could serve as a useful tool for hedging political risk and building a portfolio around your election predictions.
