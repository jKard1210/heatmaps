Track Institutional Ownership with 13-F Data

Although their hold on the market has diminished in recent years, the actions of institutional investors are often major drivers of stock market movement. Mutual funds and hedge funds now account for around 25% of all US equity trading volumes.

 

Actively managed institutions devote millions of dollars into cutting-edge data and other resources to gain an edge on the market, make better investment decisions, and generate alpha. Because of this, having an insight to their movements and positions could prove highly beneficial to retail investors.

 

Tracking the holdings of non-active managers can yield useful insights as well. In 2020, Tesla was already one of the largest companies in the world by market cap, but had yet to be included into the S&P 500. At the time, major institutions such as Vanguard and Blackrock held much smaller positions in Tesla than they did in comparable companies. After Tesla was added to the S&P 500 last December, many of these institutions were mechanically required to take a larger position in the stock, illustrating how the addition of stocks into passive indexes can be a positive catalyst. 

 

Due to rule changes in 2011, under the right criteria (Assets Under Management & Asset Type), institutions are required to submit their positions 45 days after quarter’s end in the form of a 13F report to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

Using the Dashboard

 

Ticker Search

With our institutional holdings dashboard, you can quickly see the top institutional shareholders for a given ticker, and how those shareholders have changed their positions over the last quarter.

 

Our ticker search feature generates a treemap graph, with boxes sized based on a fund's current position, and colored based on the change in that position. Pictured below is an example from Tenneco Inc (NYSE: TEN):

From this graph, we can see that Carl Icahn holds a huge position in the company, which has grown significantly over the last quarter.

 

Fund Search

You can also use this dashboard to find more details on a single fund's holdings. Our fund search feature currently returns the top 25 reported holdings for a fund, along with the estimated cost of shares that were purchased/sold of each ticker over the last quarter.
