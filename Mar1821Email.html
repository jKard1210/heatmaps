Can we use WallStreetBets to find stocks that will perform best from the stimulus? We take a closer look at the data.

The "Stimmy" Effect

162 million stimulus checks were sent last year, with another batch hitting bank accounts as of this writing thanks to the third stimulus package passed on March 10th. Most economists agree that the $1.9 trillion injection will provide a boost for local governments, companies, and individuals who face continued economic headwinds. 

 

What can we do to find the best investment opportunities that could potentially benefit the most from the government lifeline? We believe that by paying attention to discussion within retail investing communities, we can get a better idea of which stocks will be boosted the most by these stimulus checks. In this newsletter, we’re going to look at discussion on r/WallStreetBets prior to the previous stimulus packages, and determine whether there was any relationship between a stock’s pre-stimulus discussion and it’s post-stimulus performance.

 

Thanks to 9.6m members, r/WSB has massive comment volume, letting us unravel massive amounts of data and search for specific terms. In this newsletter we're going to look at what stocks r/WSB members mention alongside the words ‘Stimulus’ or ‘Stimmy’ the most, to see what effect it had and where you should be looking going forward.

Event Study: March 2020 Stimulus

In 2020, 12,500 comments contained words that referenced ‘Stimulus’ or ‘Stimmy’. These 12,500 comments were spread across 950 different stocks. To filter the list down we’ve broken the companies into 3 different categories and stated the initial findings below:

  1. Stocks with 1%+ of comments containing stimulus-related vocabulary had an average 13.88% return in the 2 weeks following the March 29th, 2020, stimulus package.
  2. Stocks with .50% - .99% of comments containing stimulus-related vocabulary had an average 8.43% return in the 2 weeks following the March 29th, 2020, stimulus package.
  3. Stocks with .01% - .49% of comments containing stimulus-related vocabulary had an average 4.18% return in the 2 weeks following the March 29th, 2020, stimulus package.

Event Study: December 2020 Stimulus

To validate this theory we've tested the same "stimmy ratio" principle above, with the comment data predating the 2nd stimulus package issued December 31st, 2021, with how the stock performed after the stimulus was passed:

The results are underwhelming: Stocks with a higher "stimmy ratio" on average did not perform any better following the 2nd stimulus. Why is this? We contribute this to 2 main factors:

  1. The S&P500 already corrected 67.88% from its low on March 23, 2020, and December 31st, 2020. With this increase, it's likely many stocks were already reflecting the price of the stimulus package.
  2. The 2nd stimulus was $900 billion, with stimulus checks of $600 per adult. Not only was that bill lower than many investor’s expectations, but it was also less than half the size of the 1st and 2nd stimulus (which were $2.0 trillion and $1.9 trillion respectively). 

How can I use this?

Of course this ratio theory isn't your 'golden ticket' to picking stocks, but taking a look at r/WSB for 'key-words' pertaining to your stock, could but useful as a supplementary method in trading/investing decisions.
